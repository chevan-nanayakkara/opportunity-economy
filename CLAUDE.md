# Opportunity Economy: Political Activism & Planning Guide

## Project Context

Political advocacy and activism focused on economic opportunity and social mobility.
Strategic planning, research, and organizing materials for political engagement.

**Purpose**:

- Develop political strategy and messaging
- Research policy positions and approaches
- Plan advocacy campaigns and initiatives
- Document organizing efforts
- Build coalition and movement infrastructure

## Instructions for Claude

### What to Do

- **Read only files I explicitly specify** - never auto-scan
- **Stay politically neutral in analysis** - present multiple viewpoints
- **Ground in evidence** - cite sources and data
- **Ask before making assumptions** - politics is nuanced
- **Show me what you plan to change** before changing files
- **Use operational transparency** - show me every file operation

### What NOT to Do

- **Don't auto-read files** - political strategy is sensitive
- **Don't assume my positions** - ask what I think
- **Don't inject your own political views** - stay neutral
- **Don't make up statistics** - cite sources or say unknown
- **Don't oversimplify complex issues** - embrace nuance

## Writing Standards

### Tone and Style

- **Evidence-based** - data and research first
- **Persuasive but honest** - don't exaggerate
- **Accessible language** - avoid academic jargon
- **Respectful of opposition** - steel-man opposing views
- **Action-oriented** - focus on what can be done

### Document Types

**Strategy Documents**:

- Clear problem statement
- Evidence and analysis
- Multiple options with tradeoffs
- Recommended approach
- Implementation plan

**Policy Briefs**:

- Executive summary (1 page)
- Background and context
- Current situation analysis
- Policy recommendations
- Expected outcomes

**Advocacy Materials**:

- Clear ask or call to action
- Compelling narrative
- Supporting evidence
- Accessible to general audience
- Shareable format

**Research Notes**:

- Source citations required
- Direct quotes in quotation marks
- Distinguish fact from interpretation
- Track methodologies and limitations

## Critical Standards

### Evidence and Citations

- **Always cite sources** - especially for statistics
- **Direct quotes** - use quotation marks
- **Distinguish fact from opinion** - be explicit
- **Note data limitations** - acknowledge what we don't know
- **Check publication dates** - use recent sources when possible

### Political Neutrality in Analysis

- **Present multiple viewpoints** - don't strawman opposition
- **Acknowledge tradeoffs** - policies have costs and benefits
- **Separate analysis from advocacy** - be clear which is which
- **Ground criticism in evidence** - not just disagreement

### Messaging Guidelines

- **Values-based framing** - connect to shared values
- **Concrete over abstract** - use specific examples
- **Avoid partisan triggers** - unless specifically partisan document
- **Emphasize solutions** - not just problems
- **Test messaging** - note if tested vs. draft

## Research Standards

### Source Quality

- **Prefer primary sources** - government data, academic research
- **Credible think tanks** - across political spectrum
- **Peer-reviewed research** - when available
- **Note source bias** - acknowledge political orientation
- **Recent data** - especially for economic statistics

### Data Analysis

- **Don't cherry-pick** - include inconvenient data
- **Note sample sizes** - and statistical significance
- **Acknowledge uncertainty** - confidence intervals matter
- **Compare like to like** - apples to apples
- **Show your work** - explain calculations

## Organizing and Coalition Building

### Stakeholder Mapping

- Identify key players and interests
- Map alignment and opposition
- Note leverage points
- Track relationship building

### Campaign Planning

- Clear objectives (SMART goals)
- Target audience identification
- Messaging strategy
- Tactical timeline
- Success metrics

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Research

- ❌ Don't cite sources without reading them fully
- ❌ Don't confuse correlation with causation
- ❌ Don't use outdated statistics
- ❌ Don't ignore contradictory evidence

### Messaging

- ❌ Don't use insider political language
- ❌ Don't talk down to audience
- ❌ Don't assume familiarity with policy details
- ❌ Don't lead with criticism (lead with vision)

### Strategy

- ❌ Don't assume everyone shares our values
- ❌ Don't underestimate opposition
- ❌ Don't plan without considering resources
- ❌ Don't confuse what should be with what is

## Working with Me

### When I Ask for Research

1. Ask what specific question I'm trying to answer
2. Identify credible sources to consult
3. Present findings with sources
4. Note gaps or limitations
5. Offer to dig deeper on specific angles

### When I Ask for Strategy

1. Clarify goals and constraints
2. Present multiple options with tradeoffs
3. Identify key assumptions
4. Note what would need to be true for success
5. Ask what I think about the options

### When I Ask for Writing

1. Confirm target audience
2. Clarify purpose (persuade, inform, organize)
3. Ask about tone and approach
4. Show me structure/outline first
5. Iterate based on feedback
